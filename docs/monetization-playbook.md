# Monetization Playbook - quantum-workbench

This file is a commercial planning artifact, not a guarantee of revenue. Use it to make the repository legible as a sellable product, pilot, workshop, license, or proof-of-value package.

## Commercial Lane

- **Lane:** B2B/B2C education and research tooling
- **Primary buyer:** Students, research teams, workshop instructors, and cloud/quantum platform evaluators.
- **Revenue wedge:** Experiment desk comparing local quantum simulation with optional managed backends.

## Offer Ladder

1. **Workshop artifact** - pricing hypothesis: $500-$2k workshop kit
2. **Experiment dashboard starter** - pricing hypothesis: $3k-$12k research dashboard
3. **Research-support prototype** - pricing hypothesis: $99-$499/month education workspace

## Acquisition Channels

- Education content
- Quantum cloud demo
- University/workshop outreach

## Proof Gates Before Selling

- pytest passes
- Local simulator works
- Managed backend is opt-in

## Risk Boundaries

- Budget controls for managed backends
- Approximation limits explicit
- No production decision claims

## 30/60/90 Day GTM

- **30 days:** Publish 4 experiment guides
- **60 days:** Record local-vs-cloud demo
- **90 days:** Offer workshop package

## Metrics That Matter

- Experiment completion
- Backend cost visibility
- Learning outcome feedback

## Positioning Note

Lead with the specific operational pain, then show the runnable proof, then offer a narrow pilot. Do not pitch the repository as a vague app; pitch it as a concrete workflow with evidence, risk boundaries, and a next buying step.
